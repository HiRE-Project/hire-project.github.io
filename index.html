TL;DR

We introduce HiRE (Hindsight Reward Editing), a training-free framework that breaks the reward bottleneck in RL policy finetuning. HiRE bridges foundation representation models with physical control awareness by contrasting successful and failed trajectories in hindsight. It identifies trap states that look rewarding in latent space but lead to failure, penalizes them, and boosts rewards for critical successful states. HiRE is compatible with any foundation representations and RL algorithms, and significantly improves finetuning performance with minimal computation.

Method

HiRE estimates a control-aware potential in foundation representation space via non-parametric kernel density estimation, contrasting success and failure buffers collected during online finetuning. The edited reward is integrated with sparse task success signals through potential-based reward shaping, providing dense guidance while preserving the optimal policy for task completion.

Comparative Results

We evaluate HiRE on RoboMimic and MimicGen benchmarks (Tool Hang, Threading, Stack Three, Three Piece Assembly), using DICE-RL as the policy finetuning backbone with DINOv2 / SigLIP encoders. HiRE is compared against sparse reward and RoboMeter under the same finetuning setup.
